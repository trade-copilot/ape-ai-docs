## Understanding Earnings Trading

### What is Earnings Season?

**Quarterly cycle:**
- Q1 Earnings: April-May
- Q2 Earnings: July-August
- Q3 Earnings: October-November
- Q4 Earnings: January-February

**Peak weeks:**
- 2-3 week period when most companies report
- 500+ companies reporting per week
- High volatility environment
- Outsized moves (¬±10-20% common)

### Why Earnings Matter

**Quarterly earnings reports include:**
- Revenue (sales)
- EPS (earnings per share)
- Forward guidance
- Business updates
- Q&A with analysts

**Stock reactions:**
- Beat expectations ‚Üí Usually up ‚úÖ
- Miss expectations ‚Üí Usually down ‚ùå
- Beat but guide lower ‚Üí Often down ‚ö†Ô∏è
- Miss but guide higher ‚Üí Sometimes up ü§î

**But it's not always logical!**
- Market expectations > actual numbers
- Guidance matters more than past quarter
- Sector sentiment affects reaction
- Technical setup influences move

## Earnings Trading Strategies

### Strategy #1: Avoid Entirely (Safest)

**Best for:**
- Beginners
- Risk-averse traders
- Long-term investors
- Small accounts

**Approach:**
- Don't hold through earnings
- Exit positions 1-2 days before
- Wait for post-earnings setup
- Reduce volatility exposure

**Pros:**
- ‚úÖ No binary risk
- ‚úÖ Sleep well at night
- ‚úÖ Avoid unpredictable moves

**Cons:**
- ‚ùå Miss big winners
- ‚ùå Less exciting
- ‚ùå Fewer opportunities

### Strategy #2: Post-Earnings Continuation (Recommended)

**Best for:**
- Intermediate traders
- Swing traders
- Risk-managed approach

**Approach:**
- Wait for earnings release
- Let volatility settle (1-4 hours)
- Enter on clear direction
- Ride the momentum

**Pros:**
- ‚úÖ Clarity on direction
- ‚úÖ Better risk/reward
- ‚úÖ Defined setup
- ‚úÖ Less binary

**Cons:**
- ‚ùå May miss initial move
- ‚ùå Requires quick analysis
- ‚ùå Early morning/late night

**This guide focuses on Strategy #2**

### Strategy #3: Pre-Earnings Play (Advanced)

**Best for:**
- Experienced traders only
- Options traders
- High risk tolerance

**Approach:**
- Enter before earnings
- Small position size
- Accept binary outcome
- Have plan for both scenarios

**Pros:**
- ‚úÖ Capture full move
- ‚úÖ Better options pricing (pre-IV)

**Cons:**
- ‚ùå 50/50 gamble
- ‚ùå High stress
- ‚ùå Can lose 100%
- ‚ùå IV crush on options

## Common Earnings Scenarios

### Scenario 1: Beat But Stock Drops

**What happened:**
- Company beats earnings
- Guidance is weak
- Stock drops 5% anyway

**Why:**
- Guidance > past results
- High expectations not met
- "Sell the news" after run-up
- Sector weakness

**What to do:**
- ‚úÖ Avoid or go short
- ‚ùå Don't fight the tape
- ‚ùå Don't average down

**Learning:**
- Price action > Fundamentals
- Market forward-looking
- Respect the move

### Scenario 2: Miss But Stock Rallies

**What happened:**
- Company misses earnings
- Stock up 3% next day

**Why:**
- Miss was expected (priced in)
- Guidance was raised
- Sector momentum strong
- Short squeeze

**What to do:**

## Risk Management for Earnings

### Position Sizing

**Reduce size for earnings trades:**

```
Normal swing trade: 2% risk
Earnings trade: 1-1.5% risk

Why: Higher volatility, binary risk
```

**Example:**
```
Account: $50,000
Normal risk: $1,000 (2%)
Earnings risk: $750 (1.5%)

TSLA trade:
Entry: $250
Stop: $246
Risk per share: $4

Position: $750 √∑ $4 = 187 shares
(Rounded to 50 shares for this example)
```

### Maximum Exposure

**Limit total earnings exposure:**

```
Max earnings positions: 2-3 at once
Max total risk: 3-4% of account
```

**Why:**
- Correlated moves (tech all moves together)
- Multiple binary events
- Can't manage too many
- Preserve capital

### Stop Losses Are Mandatory

**Always use stops on earnings trades:**

**Technical stop:**
- Below gap fill level
- Below key support
- Below recent low

**Percentage stop:**
- 2-3% from entry (tight)
- Wider if large gap up

**Time stop:**
- Exit if no progress in 3-5 days
- Don't let winner become loser
- Move on to next opportunity
